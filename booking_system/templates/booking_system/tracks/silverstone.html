About Lydden Hill

Lydden Hill is located near Canterbury in Kent and is one of the oldest rallycross venues in the world. This short yet fast circuit combines tarmac and gravel, making it a favorite among grassroots drivers and enthusiasts. With challenging corners and a unique surface, it’s perfect for drifting, track days, and rallycross events.

Lydden Hill Race Circuit is a historic motorsport venue located near Wootton in Kent, England, roughly halfway between Canterbury and Dover. Established in 1955 by Bill Chesson, the circuit initially featured grass and gravel surfaces before becoming fully tarmac by 1965.

Internationally recognized as the birthplace of rallycross, the first event took place on February 4, 1967. The circuit’s layout combines tight corners, elevation changes, and a unique amphitheatre setting, making it exciting for spectators.

Today, Lydden Hill hosts rallycross, drifting, motorcycle races, and public track days year-round.
